Martin Paulus, M.D. and his collaborators at the Laureate Institute for Brain Research (LIBR) are conducting a research study to:

  1. evaluate the feasibility of collecting data from online psychological tasks completed on Amazon Mechanical Turk (MTurk) and
  2. evaluate the test-retest reliability of tasks completed on MTurk. Approximately 200 people will participate in the study.

You are being asked to be in a research study that will try to test the feasibility and reliability of behavioral tasks administered on Amazon Mechanical Turk (MTurk).

Your participation will involve self-report measures and a computer task and take about 40 minutes to complete in each of two sessions, separated by at least 24 hours (80 minutes total). There will be approximately 200 participants in this study.

There are no known risks associated with being in this research.

You may not receive a direct benefit if you agree to participate.  However, people in the future may benefit from the information obtained from this research.

Your alternative is to not participate in this study. 

Contact Martin Paulus, M.D. at 918-502-5100 for questions, concerns or complaints about the research or if you think you have been harmed as a result of joining this research. 

Contact the Western Institutional Review Board (WIRB) if you have questions about your rights as a research subject, concerns, complaints or input: 1-800-562-4789.

WIRB is a group of people who perform independent review of research.

The study staff will share the records generated from this research with the sponsor, regulatory agencies such as the US Food and Drug Administration and the IRB. This information is shared so the research can be conducted and properly monitored.  The people receiving this information may not be required to protect it and your information may be redisclosed without your permission.  If you do not provide permission to use your information you cannot be in the study.

This permission will not end unless you cancel it.  You may cancel it by sending written notice to the study doctor Martin Paulus, M.D. at 6655 South Yale Ave, Tulsa, OK 74136.  Any information collected before you withdraw may still be used.

Your decision to be in this study is voluntary.  You will not be penalized or lose benefits if you decide not to participate or if you decide to stop participating. 

Upon completion of the two sessions, you will receive payment for your time and effort.

Session 1: (~30 minutes)

Part A: You will complete 2 Surveys that ask questions about your demographic information and psychological well-being.

  1. Demographic Information
  2. Positive and Negative Affect Schedule Extended (PANAS-X)
  3. Assessment Questionaiire

Part B: You will complete the Chicken Task:

During the task, you will be shown images on the screen of two chickens. Using your keyboard, you will be asked to determine which chicken will lay the next egg. The exact instructions will be provided to you when you reach this portion of the human intelligence task (HIT).

If you do poorly on the practice portion of the task, you will not complete the rest of session 1 or advance to session 2 and you will only be paid $1 for this HIT. The expected time to complete the surveys and practice is 10 minutes.


Session 2: (~30 minutes, completed at least 24 hours after Session 1)

For session 2, you will complete the same survey and task as session 1.

If you provide an email address, we will remind you when 24 hours have passed and it is time to complete Session 2.
After Session 2 is complete, you will be given a code for your time and effort on Mechanical Turk. 


Participation in research is voluntary. You may stop this HIT at any time when you feel uncomfortable. If you have any questions, please contact LIBR at jtouthang@libr.net.

No personally identifiable information will be collected in this study except your email address if provided. If you do provide an email address, we will delete it from the database once you have completed session 2!


Payment will be as follows:
There is a base payment of $1 for completing the HIT.
There is a bonus of up to $4 for each session based on task performance, so total payment will be a minimum of $1 and a maximum of $9.
