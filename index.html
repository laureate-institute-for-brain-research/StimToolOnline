Martin Paulus, M.D. and his collaborators at the Laureate Institute for Brain Research (LIBR) are conducting a research study to 1) evaluate the feasibilit of collecting data from online psychological tasks completed on Amazon Mechanical Turk (MTurk) and 2) evaluate the test-retest reliability of tasks completed on MTurk. Approximately 200 people will participate in the study.

If you agree to be in this study, you will complete two sessions:

Session 1: (~30 minutes)

Part A: You will complete 4 Surveys that ask questions about your demographic information and psychological well-being.

  1. Demographic Information
  2. Patient Health Questionnaire (PHQ-9)
  3. Overall Anxiety Severity and Impairment Scale (OASIS)
  4. Anxiety Sensitiviy Index (ASI-3)

Part B: You will complete one of these two tasks:

  1. Dot Probe Task: During the task, you will be shown images on the screen and presented with a red bar before and after each image. Using your keyboard, you will be asked to determine if the orientation of the bars was the same or different. The exact instructions will be provided to you when you reach this portion of the human intelligence task (HIT).
  2. Chicken Task: During the task, you will be shown images on the screen of two chickens. Using your keyboard, you will be asked to determine which chicken will lay the next egg. The exact instructions will be provided to you when you reach this portion of the human intelligence task (HIT).

Session 2: (~30 minutes, completed at least 24 hours after Session 1)

Part A: Same Surveys as Session 1

Part B: Repeat the same task (dot probe or chicken) from Session 1 with different sets of images.

If you provide an email address, we will remind you when 24 hours have passed and it is time to complete Session 2. After Session 2 is complete, you will be given a code to redeem a $3 reward for your time and effort on Mechanical Turk.

 

Participation in research is voluntary. You may stop this HIT at any time when you feel uncomfortable. If you have any questions, please contact LIBR at jtouthang@libr.net.

No personally identifiable information will be collected in this study. If you do provide an email address, we will delete it from the database once you have completed session 2!

You must complete both sessions to receive payment.
